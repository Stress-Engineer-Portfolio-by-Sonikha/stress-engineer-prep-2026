## Aerospace Metals — Mechanical Properties Comparison

| Metal | Young's Modulus (E) | Yield Strength | UTS | Elongation | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aluminum 2024-T3 | ≈ 73 GPa | ≈ 345 MPa | ≈ 483 MPa | ≈ 18% | Fuselage skins (fatigue/damage tolerance) |
| Aluminum 7075-T6 | ≈ 71.7 GPa | ≈ 503 MPa | ≈ 572 MPa | ≈ 11% | Wing spars, stringers (compression members) |
| Ti-6Al-4V | ≈ 114 GPa | ≈ 880 MPa | ≈ 950 MPa | ≈ 14% | Engine components, landing gear, hot-section fittings |
| 4340 Steel (Q&T) | ≈ 200 GPa | ≈ 860 MPa | ≈ 1080 MPa | ≈ 12% | Landing gear struts, high-load fasteners/pins |
| Inconel 718 | ≈ 200 GPa | ≈ 1035 MPa | ≈ 1375 MPa | ≈ 20% | Turbine disks, hot-section engine hardware |

**Notes:**
- Steel and Inconel 718 share the highest stiffness (E ≈ 200 GPa) — least elastic deflection under load.
- Titanium's stiffness (E ≈ 114 GPa) is roughly half that of steel.
- Aluminum alloys are the most compliant (E ≈ 72–73 GPa) — a given stress causes ~2.7× more strain than steel, which is why aluminum structures often need thicker sections or stiffening ribs.
